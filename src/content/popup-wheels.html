The Wheels

When Granny towed the popup to our house, she dropped it off with some instructions and a few things for us to checkout. Besides the obvious holes in the roof, she expressed concern for the wheel bearings. She said they probably needed to be re-greased to be fully road ready.

Bearings

My dad had also expressed concern about the tires and the bearings. He had a story about towing a fishing boat down the road and having the bearings sieze up on the highway. Needless to say, there was smoke, rubber, and potential for a lot of damage. He didn't want me to make the same mistake, so he came down and got to work on the lower business half of the camper.

These wheels are mounted to the hub with lug bolts that go through the rim, into the hub, and out of the back of the hub. Because the trailer had been sitting in a field for a few years, the end of the bolt threads had gotten rusty. This led to my dad working up a sweat to get both wheels off. After some spritzes of rust-away and some muscle power, the wheels were free.

We pounded the dust covers off and found that the hubs turned freely, still had plenty of grease, and didn't appear to be damaged in any way. We decided to forego any more work and just pack more fresh grease into the bearings before putting the dust caps back on. We might revisit this again in the future, but if it ain't broke, don't fix it!

Tires

I wasn't taking notes when Granny was talking about the tires. In my defense, I didn't think about the age of the tires until a year after we started the project. I'll spare you the boring details of worrying about whether the tires were too old for travel. Let me skip straight to the useful information.

When a tire is manufactured in the United States and officially inspected by the DOT (Department of Transportation), they recieve a DOT stamp. This stamp indicates that the tire is in compliance with DOT and includes the week and year it was manufactured.

The first two digits inside of the oval indicate the week of the year, and the second two digits indicate the year. In our case, the two tires and the spare had all been manufactured in the third week of 2019. Granny was always thorough and thoughtful, and we were ready to tow.
